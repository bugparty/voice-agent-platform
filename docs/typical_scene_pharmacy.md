A textbook case of pharmacy phone hell (minute-by-minute)

00:00 – Dialing

You call the centralized customer service line of a major pharmacy chain.
In your head, the request is simple:

“Just one question: is my prescription ready?”

In the system’s head:

“Welcome to the multi-layer IVR survival challenge.”

00:15 – Automated greeting

“Thank you for calling. Para español, oprima el dos…”

You press 1.

00:40 – First menu layer

“For pharmacy services, press 1.
For store hours, press 2.
For other services, press 9.”

You press 1 (you still have hope at this point).

01:20 – Second menu layer

“To check prescription status, press 1.
To refill a prescription, press 2.
To speak to pharmacy staff, press 3.”

You think:

“I need to talk to a human.”

You press 3.

02:00 – Third menu layer (things start feeling wrong)

“Due to high call volume, some options may be unavailable.”

The system continues:

“If this is about a controlled substance, press 1.
Otherwise, press 2.”

You begin to question your life choices, but still press 2.

03:10 – Fourth menu layer (identity check first)

“Please enter your date of birth.”

You enter it.

“Please enter your prescription number.”

You dig through text messages, the app, and your email.
Your mood starts dropping.

04:30 – Fifth menu layer (fake human handoff)

“Please hold while we connect you to the next available pharmacy representative.”

🎉
You think you won.

04:45 – Hold music + ads

🎵 Da—da—da—
Did you know you can manage prescriptions online? 🎵

09:30 – Finally connected to a person

The moment you hear a real human voice, your whole body perks up.

You get out half a sentence:

“Hi, I’m calling about—”

10:30 – Fate strikes

Them:

“Sorry, can you hold for just a moment?”

Click.

You are thrown back into the hold music.

20:30 – Connected again

Another person, clearly busier and more exhausted.

You just start repeating yourself:

“I just want to confirm if my prescription—”

21:40 – Busy tone / dropped call

beep beep beep

Call ended.
